## 📖 Chapter Content

### 3.1 What is a Prompt?

A **prompt** is simply what you type into an AI chatbot.

That is it. Every message you send is a prompt.

But here is the secret:

> **The better your prompt, the better your answer.**

Think of it like asking a friend for help:

| Bad Question | Good Question |
|-------------|---------------|
| "Help me with science." | "Can you explain the process of photosynthesis? I am in class 9 and I need a simple explanation with an example." |
| "Math problem." | "I am stuck on this quadratic equation: x² + 5x + 6 = 0. Can you show me step by step how to solve it?" |

See the difference? The good questions give **context, specifics, and clear instructions.**

### 3.2 Why Prompting Matters for Students

Imagine you go to a restaurant and say: "Give me food."

The waiter will be confused. What food? How much? Any preferences?

Now imagine you say: "I would like a vegetable pizza, medium size, with extra cheese, please."

You get exactly what you want.

**AI is the same.** If you give vague prompts, you get vague answers. If you give clear, specific prompts, you get excellent answers.

**For students, this means:**
- Good prompt = Clear explanation = You learn better
- Bad prompt = Confusing answer = You waste time

### 3.3 The R.C.T. Formula (Role + Context + Task)

This is the most important skill you will learn in AIP. Master this, and AI will become your best study partner.

Here is the formula:

```
ROLE     +  CONTEXT   +  TASK     =  PERFECT PROMPT
(Who AI   (What is     (What AI
 should    the          should do)
  be)      situation)
```

---

#### 🎭 R = ROLE

Tell AI what identity to take on. This changes HOW it responds.

| Weak Prompt | Strong Prompt (with Role) |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| "Explain gravity." | "**Act as my Physics tutor.** Explain gravity to a class 9 student." |
| "Help with history." | "**You are a History teacher** who makes lessons fun. Help me understand the French Revolution." |
| "Check my answer." | "**Be my exam checker.** Here is my answer to a 5-mark question. Tell me what I missed." |

**Why it works:** When AI takes a role, it adjusts its vocabulary, depth, and style to match that role.

**Try These Roles:**
- "Act as my Science tutor..."
- "You are a Math teacher who explains step by step..."
- "Be a friendly study buddy..."
- "Act as an exam coach..."
- "You are a storyteller who makes history interesting..."
- "Be a quiz master..."

---

#### 📋 C = CONTEXT

Give AI background information about you and your situation.

| What to Include | Example |
|-----------------|---------|
| Your class | "I am in class 8." |
| Your subject | "I am studying Science." |
| Your difficulty | "I find chemical equations confusing." |
| Your goal | "I want to score above 90%." |
| What you already know | "I know the basics of photosynthesis." |

**Example with Context:**

📱 **COPY THIS INTO YOUR AI CHAT:**
```
Role: You are my Science tutor.
Context: I am in class 9. I understand the basics of Newton's Laws,
         but I get confused when multiple forces act on one object.
Task: Explain with a real-life example how multiple forces work together.
```

---

#### 🎯 T = TASK

Tell AI exactly what you want it to do.

| Task Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Explain | "Explain the concept in simple words..." |
| Summarize | "Summarize this chapter in 5 bullet points..." |
| Generate | "Create 10 multiple choice questions..." |
| Analyze | "Find patterns in these question papers..." |
| Compare | "Compare democracy and dictatorship..." |
| Create | "Make a study plan for 7 days..." |
| Check | "Check my answer and tell me what I missed..." |
| Convert | "Turn this chapter into flashcards..." |

### 3.4 The Complete Formula in Action

**Before (Weak Prompt):**
```
Tell me about the solar system.
```

**After (RCT Formula):**
```
Role: You are my Science teacher who explains things simply.
Context: I am in class 8. I know the names of the planets
         but I do not understand how the solar system works.
Task: Explain how the solar system stays together.
      Use simple language. Give one analogy. End with
      3 questions to test my understanding.
```

Which answer do you think will be more useful? The second one, obviously!

### 3.5 The FIVE Prompt Types You Will Use

| Type | When to Use | Example Starters |
|------|-------------|------------------|
| **1. Teach/Explain** | When you do not understand a concept | "Explain [topic] like I am 13..." "Teach me [subject] step by step..." |
| **2. Summarize** | When you have too much information | "Summarize this in 5 points..." "Give me the key takeaways from..." |
| **3. Practice/Quiz** | When you want to test yourself | "Quiz me on [topic]..." "Give me practice questions on..." |
| **4. Create** | When you need study materials | "Create flashcards for..." "Make a mind map of..." |
| **5. Analyze** | When you need to find patterns | "Analyze these questions and find patterns..." "What topics appear most often in..." |

### 3.6 Three Common Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)

#### ❌ Mistake 1: Being Too Vague

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "Help me with math." | "I need help with solving linear equations in two variables. Specifically, the substitution method." |

**Fix:** Be specific about WHAT subject, WHAT topic, and WHAT kind of help.

---

#### ❌ Mistake 2: Giving No Context

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "Explain the water cycle." | "I am in class 8. I know evaporation and condensation separately, but I do not understand how they connect in the water cycle. Explain step by step." |

**Fix:** Tell AI what you already know and what you are confused about.

---

#### ❌ Mistake 3: Accepting the First Answer

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| (Ask once, take the answer, move on) | Ask AI, read the answer, then say: "Make it simpler." or "Give me an example." or "I still do not understand point 3. Explain differently." |

**Fix:** Prompting is a CONVERSATION. Keep talking to AI until you understand.

### 3.7 The Power of Follow-Up Prompts

Your first prompt gets the first answer. But the MAGIC happens in follow-ups.

| Follow-up | Why It Works |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Make it simpler." | AI rephrases at an easier level |
| "Give me an example." | Theory becomes practical |
| "I still do not understand point 2." | AI focuses on your specific confusion |
| "Test me on this." | Turns passive reading into active learning |
| "Compare this with [another topic]." | Helps you see connections |
| "Why is this important?" | Connects to real-world relevance |

**Example Conversation:**

```
You: Explain photosynthesis. (First prompt)
AI: [Answers with a paragraph]

You: I am in class 8. Make it simpler. (Follow-up)
AI: [Simpler version]

You: Give me a real-life example. (Follow-up)
AI: [Example with a plant in a garden]

You: Now quiz me with 5 questions. (Follow-up)
AI: [5 practice questions]

You: I got question 3 wrong. Explain why. (Follow-up)
AI: [Explains the mistake]
```

See how ONE starting question became a FULL LEARNING SESSION?

### 3.8 The "Temperature" Trick

Different AI tools have a setting called "temperature" (though most free tools do not show this). Here is the concept:

- **Low temperature:** AI is more focused, precise, and factual
- **High temperature:** AI is more creative, varied, and exploratory

**For studies:**
- Use focused prompts for facts: "Give me the exact definition of..."
- Use creative prompts for ideas: "Suggest 5 creative ways to remember..."

You can simulate this with your wording:

| Focused Prompt | Creative Prompt |
|----------------|-----------------|
| "Give me the precise definition of mitosis." | "Create a fun story to remember the stages of mitosis." |
| "List the causes of World War 2 in bullet points." | "Imagine you are a historian. Explain what caused WW2 as if telling a story." |

### 3.9 Prompt Templates — Copy, Paste, and Use!

Here are ready-to-use prompt templates. Save these!

#### For Understanding Concepts:
```
Act as my [Subject] tutor. I am in class [Class].
Explain [Topic] to me in simple words.
I already know: [What you know].
I am confused about: [What confuses you].
Use an example from daily life.
After explaining, give me 3 questions to check if I understood.
```

#### For Revision Notes:
```
I am studying [Subject/Chapter]. Summarize this in 5-7 bullet points.
Use simple language. Focus on the most important exam-relevant points.
Organize it as:
1. Key Concepts
2. Important Formulas/Definitions
3. Common Mistakes to Avoid
```

#### For Exam Preparation:
```
I have an exam on [Subject] - [Chapter] in [Number] days.
My target score is [Percentage].
Based on these chapters: [List chapters], create a day-by-day study plan.
Include:
- What to study each day
- How much time to spend
- When to do revision
- 5 practice questions per day
```

#### For Question Paper Analysis:
```
I am uploading my previous year question paper.
Please analyze it and tell me:
1. Which topics appear most frequently?
2. What is the mark distribution?
3. Which questions are repeated every year?
4. What are the most important topics to focus on?
5. Any patterns you notice in the question style?
```

## ⚡ Chapter Summary

- A **prompt** is what you type to AI — better prompts = better answers
- Use the **RCT Formula:** Role + Context + Task
- **Role** tells AI who to be (teacher, tutor, friend, quiz master)
- **Context** tells AI about you (class, subject, difficulty level)
- **Task** tells AI what to do (explain, summarize, quiz, create)
- **Follow-up prompts** turn one question into a full learning session
- Avoid being **vague** — be specific about what you need
- Use **ready-made templates** until you learn to create your own
